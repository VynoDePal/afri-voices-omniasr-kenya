# Data, provenance, and licensing

## What is and is not in this repository

Included:

- sanitized source notebooks;
- aggregate row/hour/word counts;
- experiment decisions and non-sensitive hashes;
- validation scripts and configuration examples.

Excluded:

- raw or processed audio;
- training, development, local-test, or competition-test transcripts;
- Kaggle archives and test shards;
- speaker IDs and row-level manifests;
- submission predictions;
- checkpoints, adapters, ARPA files, and compiled KenLM binaries;
- tokens and credentials.

## Acoustic sources

### African Next Voices: Kenya

Repository: [`MCAA1-MSU/anv_data_ke`](https://huggingface.co/datasets/MCAA1-MSU/anv_data_ke)

Used for Kikuyu, Kalenjin, Dholuo, Somali, and Maasai. The dataset card describes
scripted and unscripted speech, multiple domains, speaker-disjoint official splits,
gated access, and CC BY 4.0 licensing. Users must accept its current conditions
and cite the dataset authors.

### Afrivoice Swahili

Repository: [`DigitalUmuganda/Afrivoice_Swahili`](https://huggingface.co/datasets/DigitalUmuganda/Afrivoice_Swahili)

Used for Swahili. The dataset card describes agriculture, education, finance,
government, and health domains and identifies CC BY 4.0 licensing. Access
conditions and attribution remain the responsibility of the user.

## Additional K2 text sources

K1 inventoried more candidate sources than K2 ultimately extracted. Inclusion
required acceptable licensing evidence, a usable text field, and leakage controls.
The public experiment also considered or extracted approved text from sources such
as Google FLEURS, KenCorpus KenSpeech, and KenCorpus KenPOS. The exact accepted
source set and fingerprints are generated by K1/K2. Sources marked `review`,
`catalog_only`, `unknown`, or gated-unverified were not silently treated as
approved payload.

Because dataset repositories can change, reproduce the K1 license audit against
the current cards instead of relying only on this summary.

## Leakage controls

Development, local-test, and competition held-out texts were not training material
for KenLM V6. K2 built per-language denylists, removed exact normalized matches,
removed near matches above its configured similarity threshold, and deduplicated
the remaining corpus. Only aggregate counts are published.

## Ethical limitations

The source datasets were collected across particular communities, dialects,
speakers, devices, and domains. WER can be substantially different outside those
conditions. Do not use this model for surveillance, profiling, legal decisions,
medical decisions, or other high-stakes transcription without appropriate consent,
community context, and qualified human review.
